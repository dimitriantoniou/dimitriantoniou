### 🚀 My path here

I came to code sideways. I started as the **person using the software** — working in operations and program development, living inside the tools every day and knowing exactly where they fell short.

That turned into an informal **product role**: not a title, but the person sitting with technical teams, directing what we were building, translating real operational pain into the tooling we needed. I learned to speak the language of the people writing the code.

During COVID, I stopped just directing and **started writing it myself** — building a full-stack app to solve a real business problem, and picking up website building along the way. Once I could ship, I couldn't stop.

Now I'm a **vibe coder**, building software for a nonprofit alongside Claude Code. What keeps me going is the creativity, the flexibility, and the sheer pace that AI-assisted development unlocks — I care less about the syntax and more about getting something useful into people's hands, fast.

- 🔭  Currently building internal tools and apps for a nonprofit — CRMs, dashboards, and data pipelines that help teams do more with less
- 🌱  Always experimenting — I treat every project as a chance to learn how to do this better
- ⚡  Most of what I build these days starts as a conversation or a daily nuisance
